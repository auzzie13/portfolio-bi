About Me

I began my career in athletic training after earning a Bachelor of Science in Health and Human Performance from Middle Tennessee State University. Over nearly a decade, I worked across every level of athletics—from secondary schools to NCAA football, MLS soccer, and the NFL. My final role in the field was serving as the Clinical Coordinator of Event Medicine at Vanderbilt University Medical Center, where I led medical logistics and staffing for over 40 contracted events including TSSAA state championships.

In 2019, I pivoted into software development, completing Vanderbilt University’s Full Stack Web Development program in early 2020. After navigating a difficult job market during the COVID-19 pandemic, I joined an NIH-funded research group at Vanderbilt University Medical Center as a software developer.

In my current role, I build and manage REDCap databases for large-scale clinical studies, develop Tableau dashboards to visualize research metrics, and create automated pipelines to move data from third-party tools like Fitbit into secure REDCap environments.

I recently attended the 2025 Tableau Conference and left inspired by what’s possible at the intersection of business intelligence and software development. My unique background as a full stack developer and Tableau creator allows me to build scalable, data-driven tools that are both technically sound and user-friendly.

I'm known for my strong work ethic, problem-solving mindset, and ability to quickly adapt and learn—skills I've honed through my coding bootcamp, self-driven learning, and professional transitions. I’m excited to bring that energy to a team where I can make an impact.

Let’s connect—I'd love to hear how I can contribute to your organization.
